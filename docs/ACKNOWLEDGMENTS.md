# Acknowledgments

## Inspiration and model: Melanie Walsh, *Introduction to Cultural Analytics*

WRIT 20833 — "When Coding Meets Culture" — owes an intellectual debt to Melanie Walsh's
open educational resource **[*Introduction to Cultural Analytics & Python*](https://melaniewalsh.github.io/Intro-Cultural-Analytics/)**.
Walsh's text shaped this course's core conviction that computational methods and humanistic
interpretation belong together, and her sequencing of Python fundamentals for humanities
students served as an early model for ours.

**On dependency.** Earlier versions of this course assigned Walsh's chapters directly and
built exercises from her end-of-chapter problems. We are deliberately moving away from that:
course materials are being rewritten to be **self-contained**, so that no required reading or
graded assignment depends on an external textbook. Walsh's work remains gratefully credited
here as **inspiration and a model**, and may be recommended to students as **optional further
reading** — but the course now stands on its own materials.
